# 🔐 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## 📌 What is UPI / Online Payment Fraud?
UPI fraud is a cybercrime in which scammers trick people into sending money or revealing sensitive financial details such as UPI PIN, OTP, bank account information, or card details. It usually happens through fake payment requests, QR codes, phishing links, or calls pretending to be from banks, customer care, or online sellers.

## ⚙️ How It Typically Happens
A common UPI fraud happens step by step like this:

1. A student receives a call or message claiming to be from a seller, delivery agent, or bank representative.  
2. The scammer says that money will be refunded or an account problem must be fixed urgently.  
3. The victim is asked to scan a QR code, click a link, or approve a collect request.  
4. The scammer may falsely say that entering the UPI PIN is needed to **receive** money.  
5. The victim follows the instructions and enters the PIN.  
6. Instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment to the fraudster.  

## 🎯 Who Is Usually Targeted?
Common targets include:
- College students
- Online shoppers
- Elderly people
- Small business owners
- People new to digital payments

Students are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps for food orders, shopping, recharges, and splitting bills with friends.

## ⚠️ Consequences
The consequences can be serious. Victims may lose money instantly, and in some cases their bank details may be misused for further fraud. They may also suffer stress, panic, and loss of trust in online payment systems. This type of fraud shows why users must verify requests carefully and remember one important rule: **you do not need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.**
