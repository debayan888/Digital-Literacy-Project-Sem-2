# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online Checklist for College Students in India

> A practical checklist to help students stay secure while studying, socializing, shopping, and paying online. 

---

## ✅ Safety Checklist

### 🔐 Account & Device Security
-  Use **strong, unique passwords** for email, college portals, banking apps, and social media.
-  Turn on **two-factor authentication (2FA)** wherever available, especially for Gmail, Instagram, LinkedIn, and UPI apps.
-  Keep your **phone, laptop, browser, and apps updated** to reduce security risks from old software.
-  Lock your devices with a **PIN, password, fingerprint, or face unlock**.

### 📱 Social Media & Privacy
-  Review your **privacy settings** and avoid making personal details public.
-  Do not share your **Aadhaar, PAN, student ID, address, travel plans, or live location** unnecessarily.
-  Think before posting—your **digital footprint** can affect internships, placements, and professional reputation.
-  Do not click suspicious links sent through **WhatsApp, Telegram, Instagram, SMS, or email**.

### 🎓 Student-Specific Online Safety
-  Verify internship offers, scholarship links, and job forms before submitting personal data.
-  Download notes, software, PDFs, and study material only from **trusted websites or official sources**.
-  Be careful while using **public Wi-Fi** in cafes, hostels, railway stations, or campuses; avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts there.
-  Never share **OTPs, passwords, login credentials, or screen-sharing access** with anyone claiming to help you.

### 💸 Financial / UPI Safety
-  Before making a payment, **check the UPI ID, receiver name, and amount** carefully.
-  Remember: **to receive money, you never need to enter your UPI PIN**. Entering a PIN usually means you are authorizing payment.
-  Do not scan unknown QR codes or approve random **collect requests**.
-  If a fraud happens, act immediately—quick reporting improves the chance of blocking the transaction.

---

## 🚨 Warning Signs to Watch For
-  Messages saying **“urgent action required”**, **“account blocked”**, or **“limited-time reward”**
-  Calls pretending to be from a bank, courier service, police, or college office
-  Fake internship forms asking for bank details, OTPs, or payment
-  Links with unusual spellings or websites that look slightly different from official ones

---

## 📢 Where to Report Cyber Fraud in India
If you become a victim of cybercrime or online financial fraud, report it immediately through:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** `cybercrime.gov.in`
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930**

## Conclusion
Staying safe online is not only about avoiding hackers. It is about protecting your **money, identity, academic accounts, reputation, and future opportunities**. A few careful habits can prevent major loss.
