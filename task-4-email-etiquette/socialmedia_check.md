# Social Media Do’s and Don’ts Checklist

## ✅ Do’s
- [ ] Use respectful and polite language in posts, comments, and messages.
- [ ] Verify information before sharing it with others.
- [ ] Maintain privacy by checking account settings and limiting personal information.
- [ ] Think before posting, especially when content may affect your reputation.
- [ ] Use social media for learning, networking, and professional growth.
- [ ] Share original content or properly credit the creator/source.
- [ ] Keep your profile professional if it may be viewed by teachers, recruiters, or organizations.
- [ ] Report fake accounts, scams, cyberbullying, or harmful content when necessary.
- [ ] Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication for account safety.
- [ ] Be mindful of digital footprints, since online activity can remain visible for a long time.

## ❌ Don’ts
- [ ] Do not share passwords, OTPs, or confidential personal information online.
- [ ] Do not post offensive, abusive, or hateful content.
- [ ] Do not spread rumors, fake news, or unverified information.
- [ ] Do not upload photos, videos, or posts that invade someone else’s privacy.
- [ ] Do not copy content, captions, or ideas without giving proper credit.
- [ ] Do not engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or online harassment.
- [ ] Do not post impulsively when angry, frustrated, or emotional.
- [ ] Do not accept friend requests or messages from suspicious or unknown accounts blindly.
- [ ] Do not overshare details about your location, routine, or personal life.
- [ ] Do not use social media in ways that harm your studies, productivity, or professional image.

## 📊 Quick Checklist

| Good Practices | Unsafe Practices |
|---|---|
| Be courteous and professional in conversations | Upload insulting or damaging content |
| Protect accounts with strong passwords and privacy settings | Reveal private or sensitive information |
| Check facts before reposting or forwarding | Share false or unconfirmed news |
| Control your online time wisely | Spend too much time scrolling without purpose |
| Build a responsible and positive online identity | Get involved in hostile or negative arguments |

---

## 🧠 Final Note

Responsible use of social media helps a student create a healthy digital identity while protecting privacy, reputation, and future opportunities.
